Learning, Tools & Resources

Being "self taught" requires a little help from the community and can be overwhelming to know where to begin.

A word of advice, game studios are generally broken into very specialized roles and instead of learning everything, you should start by picking just one:

🎨 Art & Design

Concept Artist – Creates visual designs, mood boards, and character/environment concepts.
3D Modeler – Builds 3D assets, characters, props, and environments.
Texture / Material Artist – Creates PBR textures, materials, and surface details.
Environment Artist – Designs and models game worlds, levels, and props.
Character Artist – Specializes in modeling and texturing characters.
VFX Artist – Creates particle effects, explosions, magic effects, weather, and other VFX.
UI / UX Designer – Designs menus, HUDs, and interfaces for clarity and style.
Animator – Brings characters, objects, and props to life with movement.
Tech Artist – Bridges art/engineering, handles shaders, rigs, optimization, & pipeline tools.

🎮 Game Design

Game Designer – Develops core gameplay mechanics, systems, and balance.
Level Designer – Designs levels/maps, layouts, pacing, and objectives.
Narrative Designer / Writer – Writes dialogue, story, and lore.
Systems Designer – Focuses on combat, AI, progression, economy, or game rules.

💻 Programming / Engineering

Gameplay Programmer – Implements core game mechanics and features.
Tools Programmer – Works on the engine itself or custom development tools.
AI Programmer – Develops behavior for NPCs, enemies, and companion characters.
Rendering Programmer – Optimizes visuals, shaders, and rendering pipelines.
Network Programmer – Handles networking, online play, and server infrastructure.
Physics Programmer – Implements physics simulations, collision, and movement systems.

🎚️ Audio

Sound Designer – Creates sound effects for gameplay, UI, and ambience.
Composer / Music Designer – Writes the game’s score and adaptive music.
Audio Programmer – Integrates audio systems using middleware (FMOD, Wwise).

🧾 Production & Management

Producer / Project Manager – Oversees schedules, budgets, and team coordination.
Associate / Assistant Producer – Helps track milestones, testing, and communications.
Scrum Master / Agile Coach – Facilitates agile workflows for the team.

🧪 QA & Testing

QA Tester – Tests builds, reports bugs, and ensures stability.
QA Lead – Oversees testing processes and bug tracking.
Automation QA Engineer – Creates automated testing tools and scripts.

🤝 Community & Support

Community Manager – Interacts with players, manages forums/social media.
Technical Support / Customer Service – Helps users solve issues with the game.
Marketing / PR Specialist – Promotes the game, trailers, press releases, and campaigns.

💡 Good to know

Smaller studios often combine multiple roles (e.g., one artist may handle modeling + texturing + VFX).
AAA studios have highly specialized roles (e.g., Lighting Artist, Rigging Artist, Cinematic Animator).
Some studios add data analysts, monetization designers, or live ops managers for live games.

Once you pick your lane, then you should get learning.
